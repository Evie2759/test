Do you wish your city was less congested, safer, cleaner, and overall more efficient for modern day life?

With 55% of the world population living within urban areas, these citizens are responsible for consuming 75% of the resources available on the plant. As cities become more congested, advancements must be made by integrating technologies into city infrastructure to create a smarter more efficient city.


The potential of the technology:

The development of networking systems from 4G into 5G offers transformative potential to address the needs for these resources, enabling the creation of smart cities ultimately improving city infrastructure and overall way of life. Therefore, a smart city will involve the implementation of 5G networking technology as it will act as the foundation for the Internet of Things(IoT). The synergy between 5G and IoT will provide the opportunity for faster connectivity and allow for social and environmental benefits to be integrated into smart city living.

What is IoT?

The Internet of Things is a collection of networks connecting and exchanging data, often working with other IoT devices and the cloud, increasing efficiency and connectivity.

What is a Smart City?

A Smart City that uses 5G technology improves services and the overall quailty of life within an urban environment. this leads to a greater social and environmental quality of life.

How does it work?

This development will transform city infrastructure and overall quality of life. The technologies such as, 5G and the IoT, will be the foundation of a smart city. This will provide the opportunity for faster connectivity and many social and environmental benefits.





Smart City Example:

The future of a smart cities is reliant on blending technology with everyday urban life. As more people move into cities, digital solutions and the IoT allow for exciting opportunities to make urban spaces more socially and environmentally sustainable. Barcelona is a leading smart city, using IoT technology to successfully manage energy, waste, water and to improve residents' quality of life through smart meters, sensors and connected systems to reduce costs, and become the hub for a smart city future.
